IRS INFORMATION RETURN REPORTING — FORM 1099 SERIES
Internal Revenue Service / Department of the Treasury
Reference: IRC Section 6041, 6042, 6045, 6050W; Treasury Regulation 1.6041-1

1. OVERVIEW
The IRS requires payors to file information returns (Form 1099) to report payments made to
individuals and certain businesses. These filings help the IRS match reported income against
tax returns and ensure compliance with federal income tax obligations.

2. COMMON 1099 FORM TYPES AND THRESHOLDS

  1099-NEC (Nonemployee Compensation)
  - Required when payments to a non-corporate service provider total $600 or more
  - Covers: freelancers, independent contractors, attorneys, and other service providers
  - Deadline: January 31 of the following tax year (both to recipient and IRS)

  1099-MISC (Miscellaneous Income)
  - Required for rents ($600+), royalties ($10+), prizes/awards ($600+)
  - Medical/healthcare payments ($600+), crop insurance proceeds ($600+)
  - Deadline: January 31 to recipient; February 28 (paper) or March 31 (electronic) to IRS

  1099-INT (Interest Income)
  - Required for interest payments of $10 or more
  - Filed by banks, credit unions, and other financial institutions

  1099-DIV (Dividends and Distributions)
  - Required for dividends and distributions of $10 or more
  - Includes ordinary dividends, qualified dividends, capital gain distributions

  1099-K (Payment Card and Third-Party Network Transactions)
  - Threshold for tax year 2025 onward: $600 aggregate
  - Filed by payment settlement entities (PayPal, Venmo, Stripe, etc.)

  1099-B (Proceeds from Broker Transactions)
  - Required for proceeds from the sale of stocks, bonds, commodities
  - No minimum dollar threshold

3. TAXPAYER IDENTIFICATION NUMBER (TIN) REQUIREMENTS
Payors must obtain the recipient's TIN (SSN, EIN, or ITIN) before making reportable payments.
If the recipient fails to provide a TIN, backup withholding at 24% applies.
Form W-9 is used to collect TIN information from U.S. payees.

4. ELECTRONIC FILING REQUIREMENTS
Payors filing 10 or more information returns (any combination of 1099 forms) must file
electronically via the IRS FIRE (Filing Information Returns Electronically) system.
Failure to file electronically when required results in a penalty of $310 per return.

5. PENALTIES FOR FAILURE TO FILE OR FURNISH
  - Filed within 30 days of deadline: $60 per return (max $630,500/year; $220,500 for small businesses)
  - Filed by August 1: $120 per return (max $1,891,500/year; $630,500 for small businesses)
  - Filed after August 1 or not filed: $310 per return (max $3,783,000/year; $1,261,000 for small businesses)
  - Intentional disregard: $630 per return with no annual cap

6. BACKUP WITHHOLDING
Payors must withhold 24% of reportable payments in the following situations:
  - Recipient fails to provide TIN
  - IRS notifies the payor of an incorrect TIN (B-Notice)
  - Recipient fails to certify that they are not subject to backup withholding

7. CORRECTIONS AND AMENDMENTS
If an error is discovered on a filed 1099, a corrected return must be filed as soon as
possible. File a corrected return by checking the "CORRECTED" box on a new form.
Two-transaction corrections are required when the original form reported an incorrect payee.

8. FOREIGN PAYEES
Payments to foreign individuals and entities are generally reported on Form 1042-S
rather than the 1099 series. Payors should obtain Form W-8BEN or W-8BEN-E to document
foreign status. FATCA reporting requirements under IRC Section 6038D may also apply.
