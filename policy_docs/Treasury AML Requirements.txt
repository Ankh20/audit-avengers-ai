TREASURY ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) REQUIREMENTS
U.S. Department of the Treasury / FinCEN
Reference: 31 USC 5318; 31 CFR 1010.210; FinCEN AML Program Rules

1. OVERVIEW
Treasury's AML requirements mandate that financial institutions establish and maintain
effective programs to detect, prevent, and report money laundering and terrorist financing.
These requirements implement the Bank Secrecy Act and are enforced by FinCEN and
federal banking regulators.

2. AML PROGRAM REQUIREMENTS
Every covered financial institution must maintain a written AML program that includes:

  a. Internal Controls
     - Written policies and procedures covering all BSA/AML obligations
     - Designation of roles and responsibilities
     - Transaction monitoring processes
     - Risk-based controls proportionate to the institution's risk profile

  b. Independent Testing (Audit)
     - Periodic independent review of the AML program
     - Testing must be conducted by qualified personnel independent of the compliance function
     - Frequency and scope should be risk-based (typically annual for banks)

  c. Designated BSA/AML Compliance Officer
     - Must be a qualified individual with day-to-day responsibility
     - Must have sufficient authority and resources to implement the program
     - Reports to senior management and the board

  d. Ongoing Training
     - All relevant employees must receive AML training
     - Training must be updated to reflect changes in laws and typologies
     - Training records must be retained

  e. Customer Due Diligence (CDD) — added by FinCEN rule effective May 2018
     - Identify and verify beneficial owners of legal entity customers
     - Understand the nature and purpose of customer relationships
     - Conduct ongoing monitoring of customer transactions

3. RISK-BASED APPROACH
Treasury requires a risk-based AML program. Institutions must:
  - Conduct a written enterprise-wide AML risk assessment
  - Identify high-risk customers, products, services, and geographies
  - Apply enhanced due diligence (EDD) to high-risk relationships
  - Regularly update the risk assessment (at least annually or when material changes occur)

4. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD) TRIGGERS
EDD is required for:
  - Foreign correspondent banks
  - Private banking accounts for foreign persons
  - Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
  - High-risk geographic locations (FinCEN advisories, FATF high-risk jurisdictions)
  - Customers in high-risk industries (MSBs, casinos, cannabis, cryptocurrency)
  - Shell companies or entities with complex ownership structures

5. TRANSACTION MONITORING
Institutions must implement transaction monitoring systems to:
  - Identify unusual or suspicious activity patterns
  - Generate alerts for manual review
  - Document review decisions and SAR filing rationale
  - Tune models to reduce false positives while maintaining effectiveness

6. BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP REQUIREMENTS (FinCEN CDD Rule)
For legal entity customers, institutions must collect:
  - Beneficial owners holding 25% or more equity interest
  - One individual with significant management control (control prong)
  - Name, date of birth, address, and SSN/passport number

7. RECORDKEEPING REQUIREMENTS
  - All AML program records: 5 years minimum
  - CTR and SAR filings: 5 years from date of filing
  - Monetary instrument sales ($3,000–$10,000): 5 years
  - Funds transfer records ($3,000 or more): 5 years
  - CIP and CDD records: 5 years after account closure

8. EXAMINATION AND ENFORCEMENT
Federal banking regulators (OCC, FDIC, Federal Reserve, NCUA) and FinCEN examine
AML programs. Deficiencies can result in:
  - Matters Requiring Attention (MRAs) or Matters Requiring Immediate Attention (MRIAs)
  - Memoranda of Understanding (MOUs)
  - Cease and desist orders
  - Civil money penalties
  - Criminal referrals
