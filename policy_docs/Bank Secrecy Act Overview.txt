BANK SECRECY ACT (BSA) — OVERVIEW AND COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
U.S. Department of the Treasury / Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)
Reference: 31 USC 5311–5336; 31 CFR Chapter X

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), enacted in 1970, is the primary U.S. anti-money laundering
(AML) law. It requires financial institutions to assist U.S. government agencies in detecting
and preventing money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial crimes.

2. COVERED FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS
BSA requirements apply to:
  - Banks and savings associations
  - Credit unions
  - Money services businesses (MSBs): currency dealers, check cashers, money transmitters,
    issuers of traveler's checks and money orders
  - Casinos and card clubs with gross annual gaming revenue exceeding $1,000,000
  - Broker-dealers in securities
  - Mutual funds
  - Insurance companies
  - Loan and finance companies
  - Operators of credit card systems

3. CORE BSA REQUIREMENTS
  a. Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Program
     All covered institutions must implement a written AML program containing:
     - Policies, procedures, and internal controls to ensure BSA compliance
     - Designation of a BSA compliance officer
     - Ongoing employee training
     - Independent testing/audit of the program

  b. Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs)
     File FinCEN Form 112 for cash transactions exceeding $10,000 in a single day.
     Multiple transactions by the same customer must be aggregated.

  c. Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
     File FinCEN Form 111 when suspicious transactions of $5,000 or more are detected.
     SAR must be filed within 30 days of detection (60 days if no suspect identified).

  d. Customer Identification Program (CIP)
     Verify the identity of customers opening new accounts using:
     - Name, date of birth, address, and identification number
     - Document verification or non-documentary methods

  e. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
     Understand the nature and purpose of customer relationships.
     Identify beneficial owners of legal entity customers (25% or more ownership threshold).

  f. Recordkeeping Requirements
     - Retain BSA records for a minimum of 5 years
     - Maintain records of cash purchases of monetary instruments ($3,000–$10,000)
     - Maintain records of funds transfers of $3,000 or more

4. BSA/AML PROGRAM — FIVE PILLARS (updated 2018)
  1. Internal controls
  2. Independent testing
  3. Designated compliance officer
  4. Ongoing training
  5. Customer due diligence (added 2018 FinCEN rule)

5. PENALTIES FOR BSA VIOLATIONS
  Civil penalties:
  - Negligent violations: up to $1,000 per violation
  - Pattern of negligent violations: up to $50,000
  - Willful violations: greater of $25,000 or the amount of the transaction (max $100,000)

  Criminal penalties:
  - Fines up to $250,000 and/or imprisonment up to 5 years
  - If violation is part of another federal crime: fines up to $500,000 and/or 10 years

6. FinCEN REPORTING SYSTEM
All BSA reports are filed electronically through the BSA E-Filing System at
bsaefiling.fincen.treas.gov. Financial institutions must register to use this system.
