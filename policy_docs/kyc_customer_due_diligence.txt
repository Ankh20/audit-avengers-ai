KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER (KYC) AND CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD) REQUIREMENTS
U.S. Department of the Treasury / FinCEN
Reference: 31 CFR 1020.220 (CIP); FinCEN CDD Rule (31 CFR 1010.230, effective May 2018)

1. OVERVIEW
Know Your Customer (KYC) is the process financial institutions use to verify the identity
of their customers and assess the risk they pose. KYC encompasses Customer Identification
Program (CIP) requirements, Customer Due Diligence (CDD), and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD).

2. CUSTOMER IDENTIFICATION PROGRAM (CIP) — 31 CFR 1020.220
Required for all banks, broker-dealers, mutual funds, and futures commission merchants.

  a. Required Information to Collect (at account opening):
     - Legal name
     - Date of birth (for individuals)
     - Address (residential or business)
     - Identification number: SSN (U.S. persons), passport number or ITIN (foreign persons)

  b. Verification Methods:
     - Documentary: government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport)
     - Non-documentary: database checks, credit bureau inquiries, public records

  c. Recordkeeping:
     - Retain CIP records for 5 years after account closure
     - Retain verification documents for 5 years after the record is made

3. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD) — FinCEN Rule 2018
CDD requires financial institutions to understand the nature and purpose of customer
relationships to develop customer risk profiles.

  a. Four Core Elements of CDD:
     1. Customer identification and verification (CIP)
     2. Beneficial ownership identification and verification
     3. Understanding the nature and purpose of the customer relationship
     4. Ongoing monitoring for suspicious activity and updating customer information

  b. Risk Profiles:
     - Assign risk rating (low, medium, high) to each customer
     - Base risk rating on: type of customer, products/services used, geography, transaction patterns
     - Review and update risk profiles periodically or upon triggering events

4. BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP REQUIREMENTS
For legal entity customers (corporations, LLCs, partnerships, trusts):
  - Collect information on all individuals owning 25% or more equity interest
  - Collect information on one individual with significant management control
  - For each beneficial owner: name, DOB, address, SSN/passport number
  - Verify identity using CIP-equivalent methods
  - Obtain certification from the customer (FinCEN Beneficial Ownership Certification Form)

5. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)
Required for higher-risk customers:
  - Senior Foreign Political Figures (PEPs) and their immediate family
  - Foreign private banking accounts
  - Correspondent accounts for foreign financial institutions
  - Non-bank financial institutions in high-risk jurisdictions
  - Customers engaged in high-risk industries

  EDD measures include:
  - Obtaining additional identifying information
  - Understanding source of funds and wealth
  - Conducting more frequent account reviews
  - Senior management approval for account opening/continuation

6. ONGOING MONITORING
Financial institutions must:
  - Monitor customer transactions against expected activity profiles
  - Update CDD information when triggered by suspicious activity or material changes
  - Review high-risk accounts at least annually
  - Flag inconsistencies between transaction patterns and stated business purpose

7. POLITICALLY EXPOSED PERSONS (PEPs)
PEPs are individuals who hold or have held prominent public positions (heads of state,
senior government officials, senior executives of state-owned enterprises, senior military officials).
  - Require EDD measures
  - Senior management must approve establishing and continuing relationships with PEPs
  - Transactions must be scrutinized for potential corruption or bribery

8. RECORDKEEPING
  - CDD records: 5 years after account closure
  - Beneficial ownership records: 5 years after account closure
  - All KYC-related correspondence: 5 years
  - Records must be made available to FinCEN and banking regulators upon request
