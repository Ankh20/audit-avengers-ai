BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
U.S. Department of the Treasury / Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)
Reference: Corporate Transparency Act (CTA), 31 USC 5336; 31 CFR Part 1010 Subpart C

1. OVERVIEW
The Corporate Transparency Act (CTA), enacted January 1, 2021, requires many U.S. companies
to report beneficial ownership information (BOI) to FinCEN. This rule took effect
January 1, 2024, and aims to prevent the use of shell companies for illicit finance.

2. WHO MUST REPORT
  Reporting companies include:
  - Domestic corporations, LLCs, and other entities created by filing with a Secretary of State
  - Foreign entities registered to do business in the United States

  Exemptions (23 categories, including):
  - Publicly traded companies (reporting to SEC)
  - Banks and credit unions
  - SEC-registered investment companies and investment advisers
  - Insurance companies
  - Companies with 20+ full-time U.S. employees, $5M+ in gross receipts, and physical U.S. office
  - Inactive entities meeting specific criteria

3. WHAT MUST BE REPORTED
  For each reporting company:
  - Full legal name and any trade names (DBAs)
  - Principal place of business address in the U.S.
  - State or tribal jurisdiction of formation or registration
  - IRS Taxpayer Identification Number (EIN)

  For each beneficial owner (individuals owning 25%+ or exercising substantial control):
  - Full legal name
  - Date of birth
  - Residential address
  - Unique identifying number from acceptable ID (U.S. passport, driver's license, or foreign passport)
  - Image of the ID document

  For company applicants (individuals who filed the formation documents):
  - Same information as beneficial owners
  - Business address if the applicant is a corporate formation agent

4. FILING DEADLINES
  - Companies formed before January 1, 2024: Report by January 1, 2025
  - Companies formed January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024: Report within 90 calendar days
  - Companies formed on or after January 1, 2025: Report within 30 calendar days
  - Updates to BOI: Within 30 days of any change
  - Corrections to BOI: Within 30 days of becoming aware of an inaccuracy

5. HOW TO FILE
  - File electronically through FinCEN's Beneficial Ownership Secure System (BOSS)
  - Available at boiefiling.fincen.gov
  - No filing fee
  - Companies may file on their own or use a third-party service provider

6. BENEFICIAL OWNER DEFINITION
A beneficial owner is any individual who, directly or indirectly:
  a. Exercises substantial control over the reporting company (e.g., senior officer, board director,
     individual with authority over major decisions); OR
  b. Owns or controls 25% or more of the ownership interests of the reporting company

  Exceptions (not considered beneficial owners):
  - Minor children (report parent/guardian instead)
  - Nominees, intermediaries, custodians, or agents
  - Employees whose control is solely derived from employment (unless also a senior officer)
  - Creditors with no equity interest
  - Inheritance (individuals whose only interest is through future inheritance)

7. FinCEN IDENTIFIER (FinCEN ID)
  Individuals and reporting companies may obtain a unique FinCEN ID.
  Using a FinCEN ID satisfies the requirement to provide detailed BOI for that person.
  Useful for individuals who are beneficial owners of multiple companies.

8. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
  Civil penalties: $591 per day for each day the violation continues
  Criminal penalties: Up to $10,000 and/or imprisonment up to 2 years
  Both civil and criminal penalties may apply simultaneously.
  Safe harbor: No penalty if the company voluntarily corrects an inaccurate report within 90 days.

9. ACCESS TO BOI DATA
  Authorized users of FinCEN's BOI database include:
  - Federal law enforcement agencies
  - State, local, and tribal law enforcement with a court order
  - Federal agencies on behalf of foreign governments (treaty-based requests)
  - Financial institutions for customer due diligence (with customer consent)
  - Treasury Department for tax administration
