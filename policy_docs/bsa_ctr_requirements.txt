BANK SECRECY ACT — CURRENCY TRANSACTION REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
U.S. Department of the Treasury / Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)
Reference: 31 CFR 1010.311, 31 CFR 1010.313

1. OVERVIEW
The Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) requires financial institutions to file a Currency Transaction Report (CTR)
for each transaction in currency of more than $10,000. This requirement applies to both deposits
and withdrawals, as well as exchanges of currency and other payments and transfers.

2. FILING THRESHOLD
A CTR must be filed when a customer conducts a cash transaction exceeding $10,000 in a single
business day. Multiple transactions by the same customer that together exceed $10,000 must be
aggregated and reported (structuring rules apply).

3. WHO MUST FILE
The following financial institutions are required to file CTRs:
  - Banks and savings associations
  - Credit unions
  - Money services businesses (MSBs)
  - Casinos and card clubs
  - Insurance companies
  - Mutual funds and broker-dealers

4. FILING DEADLINES
A CTR must be filed electronically with FinCEN within 15 calendar days following the day of the
reportable transaction. The financial institution must retain a copy of the CTR for 5 years
from the date of filing.

5. INFORMATION REQUIRED ON THE CTR
The CTR must include:
  a. Identity of the individual conducting the transaction (name, address, date of birth, SSN/TIN,
     government-issued ID number and issuing state)
  b. Identity of the individual or entity on whose behalf the transaction is conducted
  c. Description of the transaction (amount, type, date)
  d. Identity of the financial institution and the account(s) involved

6. EXEMPTIONS
Certain entities may be exempt from CTR filing under 31 CFR 1010.315:
  - Phase I Exempt Persons: Banks, government agencies, listed companies (NYSE/NASDAQ/AMEX),
    and their subsidiaries
  - Phase II Exempt Persons: Non-listed businesses and payroll customers that meet specific
    criteria including 2+ years of operation and 8+ transactions per year

7. STRUCTURING PROHIBITION
It is illegal to structure transactions to evade the CTR reporting requirement. Structuring
involves breaking up a transaction into smaller amounts to avoid the $10,000 threshold.
Structuring is a federal crime under 31 U.S.C. 5324 and carries penalties including fines
and imprisonment of up to 5 years.

8. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
Civil penalties: Up to $25,000 per violation for negligent violations;
up to $100,000 per violation for pattern of negligent activity.
Criminal penalties: Up to $250,000 and/or 5 years imprisonment for willful violations.

9. RECORDKEEPING
Financial institutions must retain all CTR records, supporting documentation, and related
correspondence for a minimum of 5 years. Records must be retrievable within a reasonable
time period for examination by FinCEN or other authorized federal agencies.
